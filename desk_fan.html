Project Description

I purchased an Arduino kit so I could make some mini projects to occupy my time and give me experience working with other microcontrollers and components. For my first mini project, I created a breadboard desk fan given conditions temperature and joystick conditions, as well as adding a display to read the temperature and humidity.

I used the DHT11 to read the temperature and humditiy, a 16x2 LCD for displaying the DHT11's data with a potentiometer to adjust, the contrast, a dual axes joystick with button capabilities to control the speed and direction of the fan, as well as toggle it on and off. An L293D chip is used to facilitate the controls of the fan to prevent destructive feedback on the Arduino when the fan gets toggled.

Technically the board I used was an Elegoo Uno R3, but it uses the same MCU as the Arduino Uno R3, an ATmega328P, and thus can use the same code. For this project, I used the PlatformIO extension on VS Code.
